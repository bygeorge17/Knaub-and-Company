What Does a Bookkeeper Do?

A bookkeeper is responsible for maintaining accurate and organized financial records for a business. Their duties include:

  • Recording daily financial transactions.
  • Reconciling bank and credit card accounts.
  • Preparing financial reports such as balance sheets and income statements.
  • Assisting in tax return preparation.
  • Overseeing payroll and other payments.

The work of a bookkeeper is crucial for the financial health of a business, ensuring all transactions are recorded accurately and timely.

Office Manager

An office manager is responsible for the smooth operation of the office environment. Their duties include:

  • Overseeing administrative staff and coordinating office activities.
  • Managing office supplies and inventory.
  • Handling correspondence, scheduling, and appointments.
  • Maintaining office equipment and facilities.
  • Assisting in budgeting and financial reporting.

The role of an office manager is essential for ensuring that office operations run efficiently and effectively, supporting the overall productivity of the business.

Certified Public Accountant

A Certified Public Accountant (CPA) is responsible for providing financial services to individuals and businesses. Their duties include:

  • Preparing and examining financial records and statements.
  • Ensuring accuracy and compliance with laws and regulations.
  • Advising clients on tax strategies and financial decisions.
  • Conducting audits and reviewing financial systems.
  • Offering consulting services for business improvements.

The role of a CPA is crucial in helping businesses and individuals maintain their financial health and make informed financial decisions.

Responsibilities of a Bookkeeper

  • Maintaining the general ledger.
  • Handling monthly, quarterly, and annual closing activities.
